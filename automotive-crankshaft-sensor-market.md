<p><p>The automotive crankshaft sensor market consists of various types, including Hall Effect sensors, which use magnetic fields to detect crankshaft position; Magneto Resistive sensors, which measure changes in magnetic resistance for accurate positioning; Inductive sensors, relying on electromagnetic induction to sense rotational movement; and Optical sensors, which utilize light to detect crankshaft position. Each sensor type offers distinct advantages in precision, reliability, and application suitability, catering to the diverse needs of modern automotive engine management systems.</p></p>
