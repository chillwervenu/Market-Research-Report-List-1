<p><p>The gene editing service market is categorized into two main types: ex-vivo and in-vivo. Ex-vivo gene editing involves modifying cells outside the body, such as in laboratory settings, before reintroducing them into the patient, commonly used in therapies for genetic disorders. In-vivo gene editing, on the other hand, entails directly altering genes within the body through methods like injections, allowing for more immediate and widespread therapeutic effects. Both approaches serve vital roles in advancing personalized medicine and targeted treatments.</p></p>
