<p><p>Hydrofluorocarbons (HFCs) are chemical compounds that are primarily used as refrigerants in air conditioning, refrigeration, and aerosol propellants. Several factors are driving this growth, including the rising demand for HVAC systems in residential and commercial sectors and stringent environmental regulations prompting the shift towards low-GWP (Global Warming Potential) alternatives. Additionally, advancements in technology are influencing the development of more efficient HFC alternatives, improving energy efficiency, and reducing overall emissions.</p><p>Emerging markets are also becoming significant players, as urbanization and economic growth lead to increased consumption of refrigeration and air conditioning products.
