<p><ul><li>Liquid Silicon Infiltration (LSI)</li><li>Gaseous Silicon Infiltration (GSI)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Silicon Infiltrated Silicon Carbide (SiSiC) market features two main types: Liquid Silicon Infiltration (LSI) and Gaseous Silicon Infiltration (GSI). LSI involves using liquid silicon to penetrate a ceramic preform, enhancing its mechanical properties and thermal stability. In contrast, GSI uses gaseous silicon at elevated temperatures to infuse into the structure, resulting in improved strength and durability. Both methods serve applications in automotive, aerospace, and industrial sectors, fostering advancements in high-performance materials.</p></p>
