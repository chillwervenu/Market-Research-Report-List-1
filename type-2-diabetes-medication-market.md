<p><p>The Type 2 Diabetes medication market comprises various classes of drugs, each targeting different mechanisms. Biguanides, like metformin, enhance insulin sensitivity. Sulphonylureas stimulate insulin secretion from the pancreas. Thiazolidinediones improve insulin action in tissues. Alpha-glucosidase inhibitors slow carbohydrate absorption. DPP-4 inhibitors increase incretin levels, boosting insulin release. GLP-1 receptor agonists promote insulin secretion and decrease appetite. SGLT-2 inhibitors prevent glucose reabsorption in the kidneys. Insulins provide direct insulin replacement, crucial for blood sugar control. Each class varies in effectiveness and side effects.</p></p>
